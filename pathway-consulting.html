What is Consulting & Corporate Finance?

Management consultants solve complex business problems for clients across industries, from strategy development to operational improvements. Corporate finance professionals manage a company's internal capital, budgeting, financial planning & analysis (FP&A), treasury, and M&A activities.

Consulting firms like McKinsey, BCG, and Bain recruit heavily from top schools. Corporate finance roles exist across all industries and offer paths to CFO positions. Both careers require strong analytical skills, business acumen, and the ability to communicate complex findings clearly.

Core Skills

Problem Solving

Break down complex business issues using structured frameworks (Porter's Five Forces, value chain analysis, etc.) and develop actionable recommendations.

Financial Planning & Analysis

Create budgets, forecasts, variance analysis, and financial models that inform strategic business decisions and resource allocation.

Strategic Thinking

Develop long-term business strategies, assess market opportunities, evaluate competitive positioning, and identify growth initiatives.

Data Analysis

Extract insights from financial and operational data using Excel, SQL, and visualization tools to support decision-making.
