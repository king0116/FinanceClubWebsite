Investment Banking

Master M&A, valuation, and financial modeling used by elite banks

What is Investment Banking?

Investment bankers advise companies on mergers, acquisitions, IPOs, and capital raising. They build financial models, conduct valuations, and create pitch books for clients. This career path is known for its intensity, high compensation, and serving as a launchpad to other finance careers like private equity and hedge funds.

Investment banking analysts typically work 80-100 hour weeks, but gain unparalleled exposure to corporate transactions and financial modeling skills that are applicable across the entire finance industry.

Core Skills

Financial Modeling

Build DCF, LBO, and M&A models in Excel. Master 3-statement modeling, linking financial statements, and creating dynamic analyses that respond to assumption changes.

Valuation Techniques

Learn comparable company analysis, precedent transactions, and discounted cash flow analysis. Understand when to use each method and how to triangulate to an accurate valuation range.

Accounting Knowledge

Deep understanding of GAAP, financial statements (income statement, balance sheet, cash flow statement), and how transactions flow through the three statements.

Communication & Presentation

Ability to distill complex financial analyses into clear, compelling presentations. Create pitch books and present findings to clients and senior bankers.
