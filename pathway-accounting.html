Accounting

Understand financial reporting, auditing, and forensic accounting

What is Accounting?

Accountants ensure accurate financial reporting, compliance, and provide insights into business performance. Roles range from public accounting (audit, tax, advisory at firms like the Big 4) to corporate accounting (controllers, CFOs) to specialized fields like forensic accounting and tax strategy.

Accounting is the "language of business" and provides a strong foundation for any finance career. Many investment bankers, CFOs, and finance professionals started in accounting. The CPA (Certified Public Accountant) credential is the gold standard for accounting professionals.

Core Skills

GAAP/IFRS Mastery

Deep understanding of Generally Accepted Accounting Principles (US) and International Financial Reporting Standards, including revenue recognition, leases, and consolidations.

Financial Reporting

Prepare and analyze financial statements including balance sheets, income statements, cash flow statements, and footnote disclosures.

Auditing

Review financial records for accuracy and compliance with accounting standards, identify material misstatements, and assess internal controls.

Attention to Detail

Identify discrepancies, ensure precision in financial records, and maintain rigorous documentation standards required for regulatory compliance.
